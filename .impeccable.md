# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary — Design Lead at a product company.**
Branislav is reaching out to companies like FTMO, Nebius, N8n, Make, Productboard. The viewer is a design director or head of design who evaluates portfolios with a high bar. They're design-literate, impatient with fluff, and immediately recognize craft. They don't need things explained. They read signals.

**Secondary — Recruiter as filter.**
Less design-literate, pattern-matches on legibility, clarity, and whether the work looks serious. The interface itself is part of the signal.

**Target role:** IC Designer + Design Engineering hybrid. Companies where coding is part of the role — not owning the full FE codebase with its logic, but contributing code, prototyping in production, shipping interactions. Think: early-stage product companies with high design bar.

**Job to be done:** Full-time role evaluation. The viewer is asking: "Can this person think, and can they ship?" The portfolio must answer both — strategic depth in case studies, precision in the UI itself.

**Optimization target — authenticity, not viewer-targeting.** Branislav's stated strategy: *"I want to optimize for my authenticity. I believe that as such, could get me hired."* The bet is that the right viewers self-select. Don't over-target any specific design director archetype. Ship what's true.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: PRECISE · PERSONAL · CONSIDERED**

- *Precise* — every element earns its place. No decoration, no ambient animation, no marketing pad. Pixel-level discipline.
- *Personal* — a real narrator speaks from this site. First-person, self-aware, occasionally dry. Not a faceless devtool.
- *Considered* — decisions carry reasoning. Nothing is accidental. The interface communicates that the designer thinks before shipping.

**Slogan / Internal compass:** "Warmly precise. Thinking made visible."

**Emotional register:** Precise without being cold. A designer who writes. The narrator is inseparable from the craft — both signal the same person.

**What this is NOT:**
- *Not coldly calculated.* The earlier framing (Vercel/Linear/Warp voice, "more cold than warm") contradicted the shipped hero. Retired.
- *Not marketing-warm.* No "crafted with love," no "designed for humans," no feel-good padding. Warmth here is personality, not persuasion.
- *Not quirky-for-its-own-sake.* Personality emerges through voice and rhythm, not decorative UI.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Warmly precise. Editorial precision with a narrator. Rounded corners are approachable, not brutal; typography is tight but never clinical; whitespace is generous but never aimless.

**Voice references (essayist-designer-engineers with a narrator):**
- **Craig Mod** — warm precision, essayist register, designer-engineer hybrid. The reference for how personality and craft coexist.
- **Andy Matuschak** — first-person, precise, generous in explanation, intellectually serious without being cold.
- **Rauno Freiberg** — precise UIs that carry personality (signed first-person commits, distinctive micro-interactions).
- **Amelia Wattenberger** — technical content with warmth; the narrator explains, never lectures.
- **Mike Bostock** — earnest, technical, first-person. D3.js documentation and blog posts.
- **Robin Sloan** — literary + technical. Newsletter energy.

**Interface-density references (structural only, NOT voice):**
- **Vercel, Linear, Warp, E2B** — still useful as density, border, and tight-spacing references. But their voice is corporate-cold; don't mimic how they write, only how they space.

**Anti-references (never do these):**
- Shiny / glassy / glassmorphism
- Animated hero sections with parallax
- Marketing copy voice (*"Crafted with care," "Designed for humans," "Built to delight"*)
- Gradient text or decorative gradients
- Fully rounded "bubbly" UI — soft, friendly to the point of saccharine
- **ssscript.app** — chaotic layout, no visible grid, no legible typography scale, inaccessible, laggy. Fast = respectful. Heavy = self-indulgent.
- Awwwards-style award-bait portfolios — art, not UX.
- Anything that makes the viewer ask "did AI make this?"

**Em dashes:** Allowed in long-form narrative (hero, project intros, MDX case study bodies). They're part of the writing rhythm. Avoid in UI microcopy (labels, buttons, tooltips, aria, short keystatic fields) — there they read as filler.

**Theme:** Light. White base (`--canvas`) with zinc palette and 1px borders as structural load-bearers. Rounded corners on surfaces (6px outer / 4px inner on cards) are *the point*, not a compromise.

**Color:** Achromatic discipline. ~98% monochrome. The only accepted color is the project-specific "Aura Glow" — low-opacity rgba halos behind case study card images. Functional identity, not decoration.

**Typography:** Geist Sans (precision sans, tight negative tracking on Display/H1) + Geist Mono (metadata, tags, allcaps labels, status stamps). Both already defined in `globals.css`. No other fonts.

### Design Principles

1. **The interface is part of the portfolio.** The UI itself must demonstrate the same craft as the case studies. A design lead will evaluate the nav, the spacing, the hover states — not just the work thumbnails.

2. **Density over decoration.** Prefer more information with better hierarchy over whitespace-as-design. Every blank area must be intentional rest, not padding-to-fill.

3. **Personality where it earns its place.** Decorative animation still out. But warmth in voice, rhythm in copy, a narrator who speaks — welcome. The craft is calculated; the person is not obligated to be.

4. **Thinking > polish.** Case studies emphasize reasoning (*why* each decision was made) over craft (pixel fidelity of outputs). The viewer should finish reading and know how Branislav thinks, not just what he shipped.

5. **Direct copy, real voice.** Headlines are statements, not pitches. Metrics are facts. Labels are labels. But when the narrator speaks (hero, intros, MDX), first-person is welcome. No "crafted" or "passionate" or "impactful."

6. **Trust the system.** All design decisions flow from `globals.css` (tokens) and this doc (aesthetics). No ad hoc color, no inline styles, no new tokens without explicit approval.
