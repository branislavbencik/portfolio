## Design Context

### Users

**Primary — Design Lead / Head of Design evaluating a Senior PD hire.**
Branislav is reaching out to companies like FTMO, Nebius, n8n, Make, Productboard. The viewer is design-literate, impatient with fluff, and immediately recognizes craft. They don't need things explained. They read signals.

**Secondary — Recruiter as filter.**
Less design-literate, pattern-matches on legibility, clarity, and whether the work looks serious. The interface itself is part of the signal.

**Target role:** Senior Product Designer at a product company. Ideal employers treat systems thinking and coding ability as force-multipliers for a designer — not as a separate "design engineer" track. Framing the portfolio around "design engineer" would filter out of Senior PD searches; see `positioning.md` → *The Claim* for the explicit non-goals.

**Job to be done:** Full-time role evaluation. The viewer is asking: "Can this person think, and can they ship?" The portfolio must answer both — strategic depth in case studies, precision in the UI itself.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: PRECISE · DELIBERATE · DENSE**

- *Precise* — every element earns its place. No decoration, no ambient animation, no marketing language. Pixel-level discipline.
- *Deliberate* — nothing is accidental. Every decision is considered. The interface itself communicates that the designer behind it is calculated, not impulsive.
- *Dense* — high information-to-noise ratio. Respects the viewer's intelligence. No hand-holding. Direct.

**Slogan / Internal compass:** "Coldly Calculated." Or: "Cold. Direct. Transparent. True."

**Emotional register:** More cold than warm. Sophisticated without being precious. Like a devtool you trust because it doesn't waste your time.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Light Technical Canvas. Devtool-inspired — Vercel, Linear, Warp, Intercom (but only Intercom's functional quirks, not its marketing gloss). Zapier-grade clarity.

**References:**
- **Vercel** — monochrome restraint, density, precision typography, zero decoration
- **Linear** — tight grids, high-density info architecture, interactions that feel engineered
- **Warp** — terminal precision, functional technical aesthetic
- **E2B** (e2b.dev) — hard 1px borders as structure, geometric hover reveals (offset ghost cards, not scale/blur), mono accents, monochrome discipline. Evaluate in light mode since their default is dark.
- **Intercom** — quirky functional details only; never their marketing-site warmth

**Anti-references (never do these):**
- Shiny / glassy / glassmorphism
- Animated hero sections with parallax
- Marketing copywriting voice ("Crafted with care…", "Designed for humans…")
- Gradient text or decorative gradients
- Rounded "bubbly" UI — soft, friendly, approachable aesthetic
- **ssscript.app** — the specific anti-reference: chaotic layout with random items placed anywhere, no visible grid, no legible typography scale, inaccessible, heavy, lagging. Fast = respectful. Heavy = self-indulgent.
- Portfolio sites that look like award-winning Awwwards entries — the awards are for art, not for UX
- Anything that makes the viewer ask "did AI make this?"
- **Em dashes in shipped copy** — instant AI-prose tell. Use colons, periods, commas, or rewrite with a verb. Applies to all user-facing text (titles, headlines, MDX, keystatic YAML, tooltips, alt, aria).

**Theme:** Light. The "Light Technical Canvas" — off-white base, zinc palette, hard 1px borders as structural load-bearers.

**Color:** Achromatic. 98% monochrome. Color only appears inside work screenshots. No aura glows, no identity halos, no project-specific tints on the landing tiles.

**Typography:** Geist Sans (precision sans, tight negative tracking on Display/H1 only) + Geist Mono (metadata, tags, allcaps labels). Both already defined in globals.css. No other fonts.

### Design Principles

1. **The interface is the portfolio.** The UI itself must demonstrate the same craft as the case studies. A design lead will evaluate the nav, the spacing, the hover states — not just the work thumbnails.

2. **Density over decoration.** Prefer showing more information with better hierarchy over whitespace-as-design. Every blank area must be intentional rest, not padding-to-fill.

3. **Functional quirks only.** Interactions and details that reward attention are welcome — but only if they serve clarity or feedback. No decorative animation, no scroll-triggered reveals for their own sake.

4. **Direct copy, no marketing voice.** Headlines are statements, not pitches. Metrics are facts. Labels are labels. Never write "crafted" or "passionate" or "impactful."

5. **Trust the system.** All design decisions flow from `positioning.md` (claims), `docs/DESIGN.md` (visual system), and `globals.css` (tokens). No ad hoc color, no inline styles, no new tokens without explicit approval. When two layers disagree, the higher layer wins per `CLAUDE.md` → *Source-of-Truth Hierarchy*.
