## Portfolio-as-Map

**Idea.** Browse the portfolio as a 2-axis graph instead of a linear
list. Each project is a dot; click reveals a Google-Maps-style sidebar
with images and copy. Inspired by *Caps Lock* (book), Obsidian's
node-graph view, and the question "what if the portfolio *is* the graph?"

**Why deferred.** Without an *argument* to embody (à la *Caps Lock*'s
thesis on map-as-political-act), a 2D positioning matrix pattern-matches
to AI-slop. Build only after the load-bearing axes are defined and the
spatial position is making a claim, not a layout.

**Promote when.** A coherent thesis exists for what the axes mean and
why this projection beats a list. Candidate axis pairs that don't
collapse to a diagonal: *time-budget × reversibility*,
*stakes × discovery-required*, *cost-of-being-wrong × cost-of-delay*.

**Notes.** Easter-egg "graph view toggle" rejected — Easter eggs read
as indecision. If shipped, it's a primary section, not a hidden mode.

## AI-extracted design values — write the post, not the graph

**Idea (original).** Obsidian-style proximity graph of design values,
generated by AI deep-research with ICE-quantified weights. Each value
is a node sized by importance; proximity reflects conceptual relatedness.

**Pivot.** Skip the graph artifact (AI-slop archetype). Salvage as a
*post* about the process of asking AI to extract one's values —
including the chat link, the points where the AI pushed back, and what
got revised. The post embodies the transparency value the artifact
was meant to display.

**Why deferred.** Posts/articles section doesn't exist on the site yet.
Without that, the post has no home.

**Promote when.** A posts section exists, or a single-post page route
is added.

## Speed × Stakes graph

**Idea.** 2-axis chart plotting projects on speed and stakes/reversibility,
showing the range of work modes (slow/precise vs fast/iterate). Originally
proposed as a section of /about.

**Why deferred.** Plotting the same five hero citations on /about reads
as a re-explanation of the hero. Either the graph needs *new* data
(projects beyond the hero five), or it belongs in a post where the
argument can breathe with surrounding text.

**Promote when.** The site has more projects to plot beyond the hero
five — OR — a posts section exists where this can be framed with
narrative context.
